External Soundfile as Source for Granular Waveforms

  • Flowchart
  • Orchestra
  • Score 





The second instrument of this group demonstrates granular synthesis 
which utilizes a soundfile as the source for the grains.  For purpose 
of demonstration, the grains are drawn from a C major scale which 
was created using a simple FM setup.

The read pointer marks the location in the sample where the current
grain begins.  If the read pointer moves forward through the sample 
and the grain density is sufficiently high, a sort of granular 
resynthesis results.  This is the case in the first section of the 
score.  

The rate at which the read pointer moves through the sample is 
entirely flexible.  It can move forward, backward or remain stationary.  
In the second section it moves through the sample at a rate which
is ten times as long as the sample's length.  This results in a 
granular resynthesis which is ten times as long as the original while
still retaining the pitch of the original sound file.

It is not necessary for the grains to begin exactly at the position 
of the read pointer.  This instrument also utilizes a definable amount 
of random variation of start time around the read pointer.  In the third 
section the read pointer acts as the mean of a window within which a 
start point is randomly selected.  Given sufficient grain density, this 
results in a 'blurring' of the original sound.
