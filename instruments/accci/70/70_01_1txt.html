Sine Wave as Granular Waveform

  • Flowchart
  • Orchestra
  • Score 
The first section of this score file will produce a single 
stream of grains in which the base pitch is A 440 Hz.  The range 
of the random variation of grain pitch is one semitone above and 
below the base pitch.  The minimum inter-grain time is 20 ms and 
the maximum inter-grain time is 100 ms. Grain length can vary
between 20 and 50 ms. The grain envelope is defined by function 
number 39.

The second section will produce eight streams with a good deal 
of grain overlap.  The minimum inter-grain time and maximum 
inter-grain times are reduced to one and ten milliseconds 
respectively in order to increase grain density.  The bandwidth 
of random variation around A is reduced to plus or minus one 
quarter tone.

The grain overlap is a result of using different seed values for 
each call of Csound's pseudo random number generator [randh].  
Introducing some random variation into grain length and 
inter-grain time minimizes the amplitude modulation inherent to
granular synthesis.
