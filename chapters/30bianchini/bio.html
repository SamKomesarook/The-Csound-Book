BIO

I was born in 1946 in Milan (Italy), where I studied Piano and Composition under Bruno Canino, Electroacoustic Music under Angelo Paccagnini, and Engineering at the Politecnico University.Since 1974 I have been professor of Electronic Music in the Conservatories of Pescara and later Milan, and since 1987 in Conservatorio “S.Cecilia” in Rome. My interest in computer music dates 1973, and I worked (but did not produce any work) with MUSIC 360 and Music 11. I teached in a number of summer courses in Italy (Varenna, Città di Castello and Montepulciano) and abroad (1995, 1996, 1997 invited professor in Escuela Unversitaria de Musica, Montevideo, Uruguay, and in LIPM, Buenos Aires, Argentina). I collaborated with publishers Feltrinelli and Ricordi as a transator (The Oxford History of Music, Vol. X, Henri Pousseur's  La musica elettronica, Charles Rosen's The Classical Style and Sonata Forms, R.Dick's The Other Flute) and as a writer(Appendix to Oxford History of Music, “La nuova musica in Italia”, Feltrinelli-Fischer Encyclopedy) and published articles in music magazines and reviews (Musica, Musica domani, musica/realtà, 1985 La musica, Rivista IBM, Perspectives of New Music). I wrote the book Computer Music - Manuale di informatica musicale. In the domain of informatics I realized Hypermusic, a didactical software for music self-learning, and WCShell, a packages of programs to write, edit, generate and mantain Csound orchestras and scores (and other). Since 1983 to 1991 I collaborated with RAI-Radiotre (Italian State Radio) to produce contemporary music broadcasting and concerts; among them Forum internazionale (with James Dashow), a weekly bradcast on the whole world contemporary music. In 1977 I founded and conducted in Milan ensemble edgar varèse, a chamber group which repertory sapnned from Venetian ‘500 to contemporary music, and since 1992 I founded and I am conducting Farfensemble, a similar chamber group.My compositions  have been played or broadcast in Italy, France, Germany, Sweden, Finland, USA, Cuba, Argentina, Uruguay and Australia.  They are published and recorded by publishers EDIPAN and BMG-Ariola.I am a member of the Direction af the association  Musica Verticale (http://www.axnet.it/musvert) and of National Commitee for musicology (Italian Ministery of School and University).I lives and work in Montopoli di Sabina, 30 miles north of Rome. My computer skills: FORTRAN, 6502 Assembler (yes, on my Apple II!), Basic, Visual Basic, C, C++ My hobbies: mountain climbing, olive-tree cultivation and olive oil production.My family: Ambretta, wife; Pablo (29), Cecilia (18) and Carlotta (14), sons & daughters
