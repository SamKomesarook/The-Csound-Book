II. “Macchu Picchu”, film music for a non existent movie

II.1. What is “Macchu Picchu”?

“Macchu Picchu” was inspired by the reading of a poem by Pablo Neruda, “Alturas de Macchu Picchu” (Highties of Macchu Picchu), from his “Canto General”. Macchu Picchu is the well known Inca city discovered about 1910 in the Andes, at 12,000 feet. Pablo Neruda’s poem is concerned with the many men and women who build the city:

Stone in the stone, and the men, where was he?

Air in the air, and the men, where was he?

Time in the time, and the men, where was he?

I decided to write a piece for my wind ensemble and tape. It was the spring of 1993, and the firsts 16-bit sound boards for PC have just begun to appear in Italy. Naturally, I ran out and bought one, using Csound version for PC by John Fitch.

Being my first piece made with Csound, I revisited some old studio techniques of the Fifties, particularly the use of filtered white noise (rand, for Csounders) and band pass filtered pulses (do you remember Stockhausen’s “Kontakte”?).

The form of the piece is something like a bow, a bow in the time: I imagined to be a visitor in the ruins of the city, in the wind, and to see the ancient citizens come back to life to tell the story of how Macchu Picchu was built: the joys, the cruelnesses, the life on the Incas.

This introductory part is for tape alone. It evokes an almost impressionistic athmosphere, and after a while a sort of pan flute sounds in the farness.

The piece was first played in Rome, on November 21 1993, with Farfensemble conducted by myself.
