4. References

This material was inspired by M. V. Wickerhauser's work with wave packets, and his ideas about synthesizing complex signals using wave packet tonebursts. The guidelines are described in his classic paper:

M. V. Wickerhauser. 'Acoustic signal compression with wave packets'. Numerical Algorithms Research Group, Department of Mathematics, Yale University.

The algorithms were derived from some public domain Mathematica code that Wickerhauser published on the Wavelet Digest.

And the soft threshold method was taken from D. Donoho, I. Johnstone, G. Kerkyacharian and D. Picard, 'Wavelet Shrinkage: Asymptopia?'

I also invoked the average/difference paradigm, and the two-sample signal example, from the highly educative paper, Wim Sweldens and Peter Schroder. 'Building Your Own Wavelets at Home'.

To start you off with wavelets, download Chapter 13.10 of the online books from Numerical Recipes, which is an excellent introduction (lets be honest, the whole book is great) and has all the C and Fortran routines to implement a version of the algorithm.

Additionally, a very good collection of papers (all the classics by Meyer, Daubechies, Coifman, Wickerhauser, Mallat, Sweldens and many others) as well as some tutorials, can be obtained from MathSoft's Wavelet Resources site.

Finally, I must refer Amara Graps' excellent Wavelet Page, which besides from being the place I first heard about tonebursts (literally), is a well thought and carefully maintained entry point to this whole matter.
