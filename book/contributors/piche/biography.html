Jean Piché, composer, was born in Canada in 1951. After studies in Canada,
Europe and the US, his music has been broadcast and performed worldwide and has
earned many national and international awards such as a recommendation at the
International Rostrum for Composers in Paris, honourable mentions at the Bourges
International competition, two Juno nominations and a number of commissions.
Cecilia, a computer program for composition, created with Alexandre Burton, was
awarded a first prize at the Second International Music Software Competition at
Bourges. Jean Piché resides in Montréal where he is associate professor of
electroacoustic and computer music composition at the Université de Montréal. He
was Director General of the New Music America 1990 festival in November 1990.
Current interests include algorithmic composition, software sound synthesis and
vocal music. He is also very active as a video artist.
