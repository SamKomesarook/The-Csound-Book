Archer (Tom) Endrich

archer@trans4um.demon.co.uk

Date of Birth: 25 February 1942
Place of Birth: Schenectady, New York

Occupation:
Archer (Tom) Endrich is an independent composer living and working in
Chippenham, England. He lives with his wife, Liz, and has two
enormously talented stepsons.

Formative years:
The family moved to Syracuse, New York, when he was 6 months old.

Interest in music started early: his mom says that while in his crib
he used to kick his feet to the music of 'The Buckaroos'. His parents
were very musical, and he got started by studying piano with Miss Richards
and Mrs. Price; he began composing while in high school at Christian
Brothers Academy.

1965
After studies in philosophy (BA 1965, St. Hyacinth College, Mass.), his
attention became increasingly focussed on music composition.

1967-1969
While working towards an STB (Bachelor in Sacred Theology), he attended
summer school sessions at Peabody Conservatory studying music history,
theory and composition with Louis Cheslock and Stefan Grove.

1969-1971
At Union College, Schenectady, New York, he studied composition with
composer/conductor Edgar Curtis, earning a Masters degree in Education.
Edgar's profound humanity and understanding of musical form has been
a life-long inspiration.

1971-1975
He then went to the University of York, England, where he studied
electronic music with Richard Orton and composition with Bernard Rands,
and developed an individual style rooted in the study of melody, finding
particular inspiration in Arabic maqam.

He received the orchestral commission in 1974, for which he wrote
'Amoeba', a study in the pursuit of personal identity, with 2 conductors,
one of whom has an acting/speaking role, soprano soloist, and an
orchestra which speaks and sings as well as plays.

He was active as a performer in the experimental music theatre group
formed at the University by Bernard Rands. He wrote the music theatre
piece 'Savari II' for this group, and performed in it as the 'overseer',
a vocal role.

He graduated in 1975 with a Ph D in composition.

Professional life: 1975 - present
The focus for the next 10 years was on composition, receiving commissions
from Yorkshire Arts, Merseyside Arts and Midlands Arts. He is self-
employed, has also worked as a music editor and copyist for Edition Eulenburg
and Edition Kunzelman, and has taught part time at Bingley College of
Further Education, the University of York, Sheffield Polytechnic, and
the Open University.

During 1976 and 1977 he formed his own music theatre group and presented
concerts at the Edinburgh Fringe during the summers of both of those
years, featuring his 'Savari II' and 'The Burial of the Moon' (for
Bass instrument, electronic tape and actors).

He was active in the local composer/performer organisation SOUNDPOOL, for
which he wrote several compositions, and in INTERFACE, which was concerned
with discussing and performing elextroacoustic music. The latter led to
the formation of The Composers's Desktop Project, an independent company
which develops and markets a comprehensive desktop computer music system
for sound design. 'CDP' has been set up as an international cooperative
effort among composers, and Archer has served as its Coordinator since
1987. He now enjoys the use of the CDP System in his own compositional
activities, while continuing his work for the Project.
