Richard Furse is a mathematician, classical musician and computer programmer. He
studied Maths at Oxford University and Music Technology at the University of
York. An interest in experimental composition has lead to an increasing interest
in electroacoustic and algorithmic composition. Over the past few years Richard
has alternated between working as a contract programmer and time dedicated to
composition and tool development. He has developed a number of sound synthesis
and processing tools including HISS and the MN Audio Library. Particular projects
of the moment include composition, virtual acoustic spaces, second-order
Ambisonics and arbitrary Z-plane processing.
