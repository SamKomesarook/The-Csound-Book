Stephen David Beck teaches at Louisiana State University where he is an
Associate Professor of Composition, Director of the Electro-Acoustic Music
Studios and Co-Director of the Music & Art Digital (The MAD) Studio. Dr.
Beck is also chair of the LSU Festival of Contemporary Music. He holds a
Ph.D. in music composition and theory from UCLA, where he studied with
Henri Lazarof, Elaine Barkin, and Alden Ashforth. In 1985, he received a
Fulbright Fellowship to study computer music at the Institut de Recherche
et Coordination Acoustique/Musique in Paris. While his studies have
included both traditional and technological media, his recent works have
focused on computer-based interactive compositions, where acoustic
performers play with and in counterpoint to computer-controlled
synthesizers. He has received numerous commissions, grants and awards,
most recently from saxophonist Griffin Campbell, harpist Ann Benjamin,
Baton Rouge Playmakers, the Baton Rouge Symphony Orchestra, and the Misade
Trio.

His music has been performed throughout the world, including performances
at Weill Recital Hall, Sao Paolo Bienal '91, SCREAM Radio Series, New Music
America, World Harp Congress, and on the Triforium Series in Los Angeles.
Beck has also presented lectures and papers on his research in interactive
computer music at recent meetings of the International Computer Music
Association, the Society for Electro-Acoustic Music in the United States
(SEAMUS), and the Society of Composers, Inc. He is currently President of
SEAMUS.
