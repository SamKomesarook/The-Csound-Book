Michael Clarke is a composer of acoustic and electroacoustic music. His
works have been performed and broadcast in many countries and have been
awarded a number of international prizes, most recently first prize in the
1997 'Musica Nova' competition in Prague for the octophonic tape piece
"TIM(br)E". He also develops software for sound synthesis. He has spent
extended periods of time working and composing at major studios in different
countries, including: EMS in Stockholm, IRCAM in Paris and Simon Fraser
University in Burnaby, Vancouver. Since 1987 Clarke has been Director of
the Electroacoustic Studios at the University of Huddersfield (England)
where he is Reader in Music.
