Lydia Ayers uses algorithms to solve tuning and compositional problems. She
composes with unlimited just intonation on the NeXT computer and with a 75-tone
just scale on the "Woodstock Gamelan," a tubular percussion instrument. She has
extensively researched various microtonal tuning systems and worked with extended
flute techniques. She co-authored, with Andrew Horner, a book entitled "Cooking
with Csound: Woodwind and Brass Recipes." She currently holds a Postdoctoral
Fellowship at the Chinese University of Hong Kong and chaired the 1996
International Computer Music Conference.
