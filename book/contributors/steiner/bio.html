Malte K. T. Steiner (b. 1970, Germany) In 1983 he done his first computer
compositions by programming the SID chip of a C64 homecomputer. To learn more
about sound synthesis, he visited 1986 a course on new electroacoustic music of
H.W. Erdmann in Lüneburg, Germany, and gave later his first performances. The
first releases were available 1989 on tape, later CDs followed starting in 1995
with the release of '731'. In 1997 he graduates in technical informatic and
working since as chief programmer in a multimedia agency. Nine years after he
gave up his painting because he realized that painting is outdated, he shows in
1998 his first exhibition of computer sculptures and interactive art.
