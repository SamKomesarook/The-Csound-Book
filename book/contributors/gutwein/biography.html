Dan Gutwein (b.1951) is an Associate Professor of Music at The College of William
and Mary where he teaches electronic music, music theory, composition, music
cognition, and topics in jazz history.   Tango Magnetism (1998) for alto
saxophone and audio CD (soon to be released by Arizona University Recordings), is
typical of his most recent compositions involving live performers and electronic
music. Other live-performed electronic works such as Marvin's Lament (1993) and
Southland (1995) are the results of collaborative efforts with William and Mary
colleague and poet/vocalist Hermine Pinson.  He is currently collaborating with
poet/play-write Joanne Braxton on an electronic score for a multimedia play
called Deep River  (a three act play based on the theme of the "Middle
Passage").    In 1995-96 his interest in music cognition lead to the writing of a
computer-music program called Adaptive Composer, a neural network that learns and
adapts to patterns from a MIDI input.In addition to writing electronic music and
cybernetic computer programs, Mr. Gutwein music has been performed throughout the
United States and in Europe.  He has composed works for solo voice and solo
orchestral instruments, chamber ensemble, orchestra, and an incomplete opera
portions of which appear on Owl Recording, Inc. (OWL33) "New Music for Synclavier
II Digital Music System: Prelude to Act I, and "KIDSROMP FANTASIA" from the opera
"With Honor and With Dignity".  His "Reliquary for Rahsaan" for tenor saxophone
and tape is published by Dorn Publications.  He holds graduate degrees in music
compositon from the University of Cincinnati, College-Conservatory of Music where
he studied composition with T. Scott Huston, Norman Dinerstein, Paul M Palombo,
and Lukas Foss.
