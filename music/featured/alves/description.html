Hiway 70 (1997) is a work for computer-generated music and video. Among other things, the scores and orchestras included here illustrate free modulation within just intonation, as described in the chapter "Using Alternate Tunings in Csound." Included here are the csound orchestra and score files for the music of second half of the piece, which has been broken up into seven sections, each with its own orc and sco files. The resulting sound files were later assembled with a multitrack digital audio editor. This sectional approach proved to be the only practical way to work with such large files. The notes of the piece were originally written in Finale on the Mac, then saved into several MIDI files. The MIDI files were converted to score files via Bill Gardner's MIDI to Csound utility. The MIDI pitch numbers were then converted to just intonation ratios using some simple software I wrote. The Csound instruments used for this piece all modify existing samples, mostly of car sounds and electric guitars. Unfortunately, some of the sounds included on this CD are, for legal reasons, not the same ones that I used on the final version of my piece. However, they approximate my result very closely, and should compile just as well. They are described below:

BassA3.aiff A sample of an electric bass playing the frequency 113 hz.
BassExtA3.aiff The same electric bass sample but now time-stretched to extend the decay without changing the pitch.
carhorn.aiff A sample of a car horn with a fundamental at 376 hz. A prominent tone about a major third up is also present in my sample (and most car horns).
disgtrA4.aiff A sample of an electric guitar playing with a "fuzz box" kind of distortion. The frequency is 113 hz.
glass.aiff A sample of glass shattering. There is a lot of noise here, of course, but in my sample there is a prominent break at 3520 hz once.
glockA5.aiff A sample of a glockenspiel playing 1760 hz.
Gong.aiff A sample of a Javanese gong at 55 hz.
gtrA4.aiff A sample of an electric guitar playing the pitch 223 hz.
gtrvcfs4.aiff For this sample, I took the above guitar sample, shifted the pitch up to 750 hz, and convolved it with a soprano voice singing that pitch, using a separate Csound instrument and the CVANAL preprocessor. The result is a kind of cross between the two.
LoCarDr.aiff For this sound, I took a sample of a slamming car door and modified it so that it sounds somewhat like a drum.
MidCarDr.aiff The same as the above, but with a somewhat higher pitch.
traffic.aiff This is a long sample of traffic sounds recorded from the side of a hiway. There is a natural amplitude peak about 7.5 seconds into the sound when a car whizzes by.
warpgtrfs4.aiff This is the same guitar sample as above, but time-shifted so that the decay is much longer and pitch-shifted up to 750 hz.
